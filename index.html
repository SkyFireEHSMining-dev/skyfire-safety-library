Field Safety Resources

Safety resources, guidance, and field-proven insights from industry experts.

Powered by MSHA Safety Services Inc.

Training, documents, practical guidance, and expert-backed field support

This module is designed to make useful mine safety information easier to access in the field. The goal is to surface practical resources, trusted guidance, and field-proven insights that help crews make stronger decisions.

It is not meant to replace expert support. It is meant to increase access to helpful information and make it easier for users to recognize when they should reach out to a professional for deeper help.

Safety Program Tools

Offline tools for audits, inspections, templates, and tracking.

Powered by Lynx Global Intelligence

AI-driven HSEC workflows for smarter, safer operations

This module is designed to give field teams useful, offline-friendly tools that help them assess readiness, organize work, and recognize when deeper professional support may be needed.

The goal is not to replace a full professional platform. The goal is to provide practical tools that create value in the field and encourage users to seek stronger safety systems when they are ready.

Safety Audit Tool (Lite)

A simple self-assessment that helps users score safety and compliance readiness, identify weak areas, and see where improvement is needed.

Inspection Checklist Builder

A quick builder for printable inspection checklists based on equipment, work areas, or common site categories like mills, pits, electrical systems, and mobile equipment.

Printable Safety Templates

Field-ready forms such as inspection sheets, hazard logs, incident forms, and quick documentation templates that crews can actually use.

Basic Safety Dashboard (Lite)

A simple input tool where users can enter hazards, inspections, incidents, or actions and get a quick visual snapshot of site safety activity.

Environmental / Exposure Awareness

A prompt-based or calculator-style tool to help users think through environmental concerns, exposure risks, and site-specific awareness items before they become bigger problems.
